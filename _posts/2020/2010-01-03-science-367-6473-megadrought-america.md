## Past megadroughts hit North and South America in tandem

[For 10 years, central Chile has been gripped by unrelenting drought. With 30% less rainfall than normal, verdant landscapes have withered, reservoirs are low, and more than 100,000 farm animals have died. The dry spell has lasted so long that researchers are calling it a “megadrought,” rivaling dry stretches centuries ago. It's not so different from the decadelong drought that California, some 8000 kilometers away, endured until last year.](DOI: 10.1126/science.367.6473.10)

By Paul Voosen | Jan 3rd, 2020
